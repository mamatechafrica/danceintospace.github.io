About Us

Where Tradition Meets Contemporary Expression

Dance Into Space is a vibrant hub for performing arts, celebrating the beauty of dance and theatre as tools for entertainment, education, and community connection. Rooted in the richness of traditional vocabularies and folk media, we've crafted a distinctive contemporary dance-theatre language. Through this innovative approach, we create performances that inspire, educate, and advocate for meaningful change.

Dance Into Space (DIS) is a premier mixed-able performing arts company, focused on contemporary dance, under the artistic direction of Ondiege Matthew.

Mission

To train artists in dance theatre and produce high-quality works for entertainment, personal development and advocacy.

Vision

To have a society where dance and theatre are embraced and utilized by everyone in all areas of life.

Frequently asked questions

What has Dance Into Space done in the past that brought about positive change in society?
Dance Into Space has trained nearly 100 dancers with disabilities and established new mixed-ability groups in Kenya. The organization has decentralized contemporary dance by creating new groups, expanding audiences, and establishing performance and training spaces in new areas.
What impact has the inclusive dance initiative had on the acceptance of differences and the arts community?
Universally, dance has a niche that has been carved as 'normal'. Anything beyond this niche is regarded as a shift towards a different direction. However, it has been working well for the past few years, and project results borne of this specific intervention have seen an increased measure of outcomes with reference to acceptance of differences, demystifying of myths, breaking taboos, and misconceptions, and creating new communities of creatives in inclusive arts.
What is the underlying philosophy behind emphasizing accessibility in the arts, particularly in dance?
To question the existing educational and cultural conditions by emphasizing accessibility as a process and a way of creating connection between people with and without disabilities and a way of knowing and being in the world through art that is dance.
